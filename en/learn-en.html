What is "Not Tested on Animals" (Cruelty‑Free)?

"Not tested on animals" refers to a testing policy (final product + ingredients + suppliers). On this site, the filtering is double — we only present what is both 100% Vegan and Not Tested on Animals.

Why is it important to check certifications?

Because not everyone who writes "we don't test" actually meets the standard. A good certification is usually a process involving inspections, documentation, and renewal.

Vegan ≠ Not Tested on Animals

Vegan = No animal-derived ingredients. Not Tested on Animals = No animal testing. These can overlap – but not always.

✅ Practical Tip: Look for "Not Tested on Animals" first, then check if it's also Vegan (if that's important to you).

Important Differences: Leaping Bunny / CFI vs. PETA

The difference between these organizations is significant – mainly in the level of supervision and the verification process brands undergo.

Feature Leaping Bunny / CFI PETA
Reliability Level Very High (external audits) Medium (statement only)
Supplier Check Mandatory (documented monitoring system) General statement by the brand
Audits Yes, regularly No
Annual Renewal Yes Not always (usually a one-time statement)
Reach International (via CFI) International

1) Leaping Bunny

Considered the "gold standard" in the cruelty‑free world.

  • Strict Verification: Not just a statement – requires a supplier monitoring system and consent to external audits.
  • Fixed Cut‑off Date: The company sets a date from which point forward it and its suppliers do not perform tests.
  • Renewal: A commitment renewed every year.

2) Cruelty Free International (CFI)

This is the global body (UK) that operates the Leaping Bunny program outside of North America.

  • Simply put: When you see a Leaping Bunny logo on an international brand – often the approval comes through CFI.
  • Why is it confusing? leapingbunny.org mainly shows brands from the US branch; many international brands will appear specifically on the CFI website.

3) PETA (Beauty Without Bunnies)

A larger list because it's easier to join: usually involves a questionnaire + a signed statement of assurance (without independent audits of suppliers/factories).

  • Advantage: Many brands are available on the list.
  • Disadvantage: Less external supervision compared to Leaping Bunny / CFI.
  • There is also a vegan version of PETA (Not Tested on Animals + Vegan).
